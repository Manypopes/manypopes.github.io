Computer Case Ducting

2017

In my final year at University I designed and produced custom 3D printed ducting to aid airflow through a Fractal Design Node202 computer case.

The case is well known for being extremely compact, but also for having poor cooling performance resulting in high GPU temperatures.

Without any ducting, hot exhaust air within the case is recirculated into the cooling fans, resulting in less-than-optimal cooling.
By incorporating ducts, hot air is no longer recirculated. Instead, air is pulled exclusively from outside through the wall vents.
The wall vents on the outer casing, with dust filter and ducting beneath.
As cool air is pulled into the case through the ducting, hot air is forced to leave through side and rear vents.
The two ducts were designed in Autodesk Fusion 360. Firstly the relevant geometries of the graphics card and case were modelled separately and then assembled together. The ducts were then designed to fit the space which remained.
Each duct was produced using FDM 3D printing. The printer used was a Prusa Mk1, with Floreon PLA filament.
The two ducts are individual, and also leave clearance for cables and the case's central support arm. M3 countersunk screws are directly tapped into the plastic in order to secure them.
